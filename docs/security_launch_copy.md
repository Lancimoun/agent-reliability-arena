## LinkedIn Long Post

I turned a real account security incident into a sanitized public field report.

The biggest lesson:

Changing your password is not enough if an attacker planted OAuth or passkey persistence.

Most people know to reset passwords after a compromise. Fewer people know to check the parts that survive a password reset:

- connected apps / OAuth grants
- passkeys and security keys
- active sessions and trusted devices
- mail filters and forwarding rules
- recovery email and recovery phone paths
- backup codes and account protection settings

That was the part that changed how I think about security.

The obvious failure mode was unauthorized account activity. The hidden failure mode was persistence: a backdoor that could keep working even after the password changed.

So I wrote a sanitized field report and built a free browser-only checklist.

It helps people score their own account security posture across:

- unique passwords
- password manager usage
- two-factor coverage
- authenticator/passkey strength
- OAuth connected-app review
- active session review
- recovery account hardening
- offline backup codes

Nothing is uploaded. The checklist runs locally in the browser.

This connects directly to my work on AI reliability.

Trustworthy systems are not just impressive demos. They need failure-mode thinking, recovery paths, visibility, and verification.

That is the same mindset behind Agent Reliability Arena: test the hidden failure modes before users discover them the hard way.

I am building in public, learning in public, and turning painful lessons into tools that help others build safer systems.
